Comparison of cost of personal and public transportation

Comparing the total energy use and additional costs of a diesel bus and a personal automobile, the bus is cheaper in an urban environment at peak hours (rush hour).

At peak hours in an urban environment the average cost per passenger mile for a diesel bus is $0.295 versus $0.464 for a personal car.

In an urban environment at non-peak hours the car is cheaper per passenger mile: a car is $0.172 while the diesel bus is $0.340.

In an urban environment at non-peak hours the car is cheaper per passenger mile: a car is $0.172 while the diesel bus is $0.340.

But these costs do not account for economies of scale and the ridership of public transport. Average occupancy of a car is a little more than 1 while average occupancy of a bus is 25. Taking all this into account the bus costs much less to the individual and to the public.

The per capita annual external costs of a public bus versus a personal vehicle is .3 to 1.

This data was taken from Victoria Transport Policy Institute (Tables 40,41).
